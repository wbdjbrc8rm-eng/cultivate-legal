Cultivate — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2, 2026

Cultivate ("the app", "we", "us") is a garden-planning app for Android. This policy explains what information the app collects, why, and how it's handled. We built Cultivate to work with as little personal data as possible, and to be upfront about the rest.

Summary

  • Your garden data (beds, plants, notes) is stored on your device by default.
  • Cloud backup is optional — it only happens if you create an account.
  • We do not sell your data, and we do not run ads or third-party trackers/analytics in the app.
  • Location is used only to give you accurate weather, sun, and planting-zone information for your own garden.

Information we collect

DataWhenWhy
Precise or approximate location When you grant location permission, or type an address To find your property on the map, determine your local weather, frost dates, and USDA hardiness zone
Garden data — bed layout, plant list, planting dates, notes, photos you submit for diagnosis, chat messages you send to the Garden Advisor As you use the app Core app functionality — tracking your garden, sun exposure, watering, and AI features

Device notification token If you allow notifications To send you local reminders (watering, frost alerts) generated on your device

We do not collect your name, contacts, camera roll (beyond the specific photo you choose to submit for plant diagnosis), or any data unrelated to the features above.

How your data is stored

  • On your device: your garden layout, plants, and preferences are stored locally in the app's database, protected by standard Android app-sandboxing (no other app can read it). This local database is not separately encrypted beyond that sandboxing. This is the default for every user.
  • In the cloud (optional): if you create an account (Profile → Account & Backup), a copy of your garden data is stored with our backend provider, Supabase, so you can restore it on a new device. This only happens if you explicitly sign up.

Third-party services we use

Cultivate relies on a small number of service providers to function. Each only receives the minimum data needed for its specific purpose:

  • Mapbox — powers the map and address search. Your searched address and approximate location are sent to Mapbox to render the map and find your property. (Mapbox Privacy Policy)
  • Supabase — our backend provider, used for optional account sign-in, optional cloud backup storage, and to relay AI requests (see below). (Supabase Privacy Policy)
  • Anthropic (Claude) — powers the optional "Garden Advisor" chat and "Diagnose" photo-analysis features. When you use these features, your message text, a snapshot of your garden data, and/or the photo you submit are sent (via our server) to Anthropic to generate a response. Anthropic does not use this data to train its models. (Anthropic Privacy Policy)

We do not use advertising networks, and we do not integrate any analytics or crash-reporting SDK that shares data with third parties beyond what's described above.

AI-generated content

The "Garden Advisor" chat and "Diagnose" photo-analysis features are powered by an AI model (Anthropic's Claude). Responses — including plant identification, health diagnoses, and care recommendations — are AI-generated, not reviewed by a human before you see them, and can be incorrect. They are not a substitute for professional agronomic, horticultural, or plant-pathology advice. Use your own judgment, especially for anything with real consequences for your plants or property. We do not use your prompts or photos to train Anthropic's models.

Data retention and deletion

Local garden data stays on your device until you uninstall the app or clear its storage. If you've created a cloud-backup account, you can delete your account and its backed-up data by contacting us at the email below. We do not have a self-service delete-account button in the app yet — requests are handled manually and completed within 30 days.

Your rights (GDPR — EEA/UK users)

If you're in the EEA or UK, you have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or export (receive a copy of) your personal data, and to object to our processing of it. To exercise any of these, email us at the address below — we do not currently have automated self-service tools for these requests, so they're handled manually, and we'll respond within one month as required by law. The lawful basis for processing is your consent (creating an account, granting location permission) or our legitimate interest in providing the core app functionality you're actively using (e.g. showing your garden on a map you asked to see).

Your rights (California — CCPA/CPRA)

California residents have the right to know what personal information we collect, to request its deletion, and to non-discrimination for exercising these rights (contact us at the email below). We do not sell or share personal information as defined under CCPA/CPRA — we don't use advertising networks, behavioral-advertising trackers, or data brokers, so there is currently no "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" mechanism to opt out of, because no such sharing happens.

Children's privacy

Cultivate is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Security

Data in transit to our services is encrypted (HTTPS/TLS). Cloud-stored garden backups are access-controlled so only your signed-in account can read or write them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the updated policy.
